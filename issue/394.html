Arizona changed their death reporting methodology 5/8

Issue number 394

Lambrettaman opened this issue on May 8, 2020 at 10:51 am

Open in Github

Sorry if this isn’t the right place to post this.

AZ changed their death reporting methodology on 5/8. The data source is different and they are now using it to tie the death to the date of death, not the date of reporting the death. (Oddly enough, because of the time lag for reporting it makes the untrained eye think that the deaths per day have peaked… but I digress). They reported 67 new deaths today, but 35 of them date back as far as 12 April. [https://directorsblog.health.azdhs.gov/identifying-covid-19-deaths-in-arizona/]

Also, they implemented a huge change in the testing approach last weekend which increased the number of tests done, increased the number of cases (fancy that), and allows them to say that the positive test rate dropped significantly.

As an AZ resident and user of their data, I also question your A+ rating of their data
