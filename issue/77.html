No, it’s that the county data itself is extremely hard to understand and wrangle and verify independently.

The Times has been great, and we think their organization has done a fantastic job.

We pull from the states and we (and you) can check against what the states are saying. The Times is pulling from a wider variety of sources, not all of which we have access to. So, let’s say that if you roll up their county-by-county case counts and compare it to a state. Those numbers might be different for a lot of reasons. We can’t really say why and that makes it hard to adjudicate what number to use.

Does that make sense?

Alexis
