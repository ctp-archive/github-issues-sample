olivierlacan commented on April 4, 2020 at 9:36 am
Open in Github

Case dates are routinely back-filled resulting in updates to this daily delta bar chart (which I’m guessing you’re using as a source here) when they’re processed by FDOH so their daily delta will change over time whereas we capture the daily state of total positives, negatives, and pending cases each day so we’re bound to have fewer if they retroactively add cases that were first processed (by their definition I believe it’s when the positive test first came back).

The more tests are done the more they’ll have a backlog. There’s not much we can do other than re-create time series aggregates (cases grouped by day of diagnosis) but even that is imperfect because patients who die don’t die on the day of their diagnosis which is all the Florida data gives as far as dates.

As methodology goes the two methods are valid. We’re capturing known cases on any given day. FDOH is doing that too but adding and removing cases (erroneously counted deaths or false positives that are later removed) to previously released data.
