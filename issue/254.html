Reported death date versus actual death date

Issue number 254

ssilver31 opened this issue on April 22, 2020 at 9:55 am

Open in Github

There appears to be discrepancy in deaths per day between the reported date and the actual date. Part of this is timing and part might stem from the CDC April 14 update on “probable” deaths. For example, when NYC reported 3,338 “probable” deaths and states are now reporting previously unrecorded deaths outside of hospitals (nursing homes, etc) how are these being accounted for: on the day they are reported or on the prior dates when they occured? Or both depending on the data format?

For example Massachusetts includes a daily chart of daily and total deaths (see attached). The footnote reads: “*Counts on the trend chart do not match total number of deaths reported, as there is a several day lag in reporting by date of death.” (https://www.mass.gov/doc/covid-19-dashboard-april-21-2020/download).

The daily deaths are much lower than the deaths reported on the website and most data sources. Are they waiting on the actual dates to be determined before adding them in a time-series? Is this the delay they are talking about?
