My local county’s data show a fairly flat rate of new cases, but a decreasing fraction of fatalities. I suspect this is partially because a larger fraction of non-fatal cases are being identified now than a month ago.

If this is the case then the social distancing mitigation may have been more effective than it appears, and the actual number of new cases may be declining rather than staying flat (but noisy).

Has this issue been discussed, and if so is it possible to extract some information about it?
